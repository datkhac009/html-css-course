Classic Japanese Izakaya

Classic Japanese Izakaya
KODO Toshishito is the younger "brother" of
the KODO restaurant of modern Japanese cuisine, which is located in the legendary Moscow
cinema near Red Square and feeds the most sophisticated audience
of the capital
and its guests for over 6 years.

The KODO restaurant appeared in the fall of
2016 on the territory of the Moscow cinema complex and has been serving the most
sophisticated audience of the capital ever since. KODO was created
by Oleg Krymasov, who designed the
space and made most of the
recipes.

The start for KODO was the Moscow Gastronomic Festival in 2016,
where the restaurant received the Grand Prix and the title of Bestseller
- more than 3,000 sets were sold in one month, which is an absolute
record for 10 years of the festival's existence. In 2019, KODO
triumphantly repeated its success, once again becoming the winner
of this festival.

The Kodo Toshishito restaurant on Novorizhskoye Shosse was
opened in the fall of 2020 and is the first in a small chain of
restaurants offering a completely new level of delivery and to go
service. The "little brother" KODO is less prudish and campy, and is a
classic Japanese izakaya with huge aquariums and a Japanese
robata grill.
